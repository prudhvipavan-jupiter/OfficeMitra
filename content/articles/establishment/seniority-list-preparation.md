## Overview

Guidelines for preparing, publishing, and finalizing seniority lists for AP government employees as per Rule 27 of AP State and Subordinate Service Rules, including inter-se seniority and objection handling.

At Community Health Centre, Markapur, the Superintendent directed preparation of a seniority list for Pharmacists (5 incumbents). Smt. Lakshmi prepared the draft using date of joining in the category as the primary criterion. One objection was received from Sri Venkatesh claiming seniority based on earlier temporary service.

## Applicable Rules

- AP State and Subordinate Service Rules, 1996 — Rule 27
- AP State and Subordinate Service Rules — Rule 28
- Category-specific recruitment rules (direct vs promotees)
- AP Reservation Rules for roster seniority
- Instructions on preparation of common seniority lists

## Government Orders

Rule 27 of AP State and Subordinate Service Rules on [GOIR](https://goir.ap.gov.in/). G.O.Ms. on common seniority lists and counting of temporary service. Department-specific seniority instructions for Medical and Health categories issued by Commissioner of Health.

## Procedure

1. Collect date of birth, date of entry into service, and appointment order details for all incumbents
2. Classify employees as direct recruits, promotees, or appointees under special rules
3. Apply Rule 27 principles to determine inter-se seniority within each category
4. Prepare draft seniority list in serial order with relevant dates shown
5. Publish draft list inviting objections within prescribed period (typically 15 days)
6. Scrutinize objections received and pass speaking orders on each
7. Finalize seniority list after objection period and issue authenticated copy
8. Maintain seniority register and update on every new appointment or promotion

## Checklist

- [ ] Complete data collected for all employees in the category
- [ ] Direct recruit vs promotee classification verified
- [ ] Date of commencement of probation/joining correctly recorded
- [ ] Draft list published with objection deadline notified
- [ ] All objections disposed with speaking orders
- [ ] Final list signed by competent authority
- [ ] Copies circulated to all concerned employees
- [ ] Seniority register updated and preserved for audit

## Practical Example

At Community Health Centre, Markapur, the Superintendent directed preparation of a seniority list for Pharmacists (5 incumbents). Smt. Lakshmi prepared the draft using date of joining in the category as the primary criterion. One objection was received from Sri Venkatesh claiming seniority based on earlier temporary service.

After verification of temporary service counting rules, the objection was rejected vide Proc.No. 78/Estt/2025. The final seniority list was published on 01-12-2025 and used for the subsequent promotion panel.

## Common Audit Objections

1. Seniority prepared without considering Rule 27 principles
2. Temporary service counted without rule authority
3. Objections not invited or not disposed properly
4. Seniority list not updated after new appointments
5. Inter-se seniority between direct recruits and promotees incorrect
