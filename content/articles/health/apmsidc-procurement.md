## Overview

APMSIDC (AP Medical Services and Infrastructure Development Corporation) procurement workflow for drugs, equipment, and infrastructure — indent process, rate contracts, and supply tracking for hospitals.

DH Kurnool pharmacy submitted quarterly drug indent for Rs. 12,00,000 to DMHO Kurnool. Consolidated with other hospitals and forwarded to APMSIDC on 01-01-2026.

## Applicable Rules

- APMSIDC Act and procurement guidelines
- Health Department drug procurement policy G.Os
- APMSIDC rate contract procedures
- Essential Drugs List (EDL) compliance
- Store purchase and inventory management for APMSIDC supplies

## Government Orders

APMSIDC procurement guidelines on APMSIDC portal. Health Department drug policy G.O.Ms. on [GOIR](https://goir.ap.gov.in/) and [health.ap.gov.in](https://health.ap.gov.in/).

## Procedure

1. Hospital prepares drug/equipment indent based on consumption pattern and EDL
2. Submit indent to DMHO/DHO for consolidation and approval
3. DMHO forwards consolidated indent to APMSIDC through online portal
4. APMSIDC processes indent against rate contracts and issues supply order
5. Track supply delivery through APMSIDC portal and acknowledge receipt
6. Inspect received goods — quality, quantity, expiry dates for drugs
7. Enter in stock register and issue to pharmacy/departments
8. Process payment/acknowledgment and report supply gaps to APMSIDC

## Checklist

- [ ] Indent prepared based on EDL and consumption data
- [ ] DMHO approval obtained on indent
- [ ] Indent submitted through APMSIDC online portal
- [ ] Supply order tracked on APMSIDC portal
- [ ] Goods inspected on receipt — quality and expiry verified
- [ ] Stock register updated with batch numbers and expiry dates
- [ ] Issue to pharmacy/departments documented
- [ ] Supply gaps/shortages reported to APMSIDC

## Practical Example

DH Kurnool pharmacy submitted quarterly drug indent for Rs. 12,00,000 to DMHO Kurnool. Consolidated with other hospitals and forwarded to APMSIDC on 01-01-2026.

APMSIDC supply order SO-2026-452 issued. 85% drugs received by 15-02-2026. Smt. Lakshmi verified invoices, entered in stock register with batch/expiry. Remaining 15% shortage reported through APMSIDC grievance portal.

## Service Register Entry

Not applicable — APMSIDC procurement is institutional store/pharmacy record.

## Common Audit Objections

1. Drugs procured outside APMSIDC without exemption
2. Expired drugs received but not returned/rejected
3. Stock register not updated with APMSIDC supply
4. Indent not based on EDL or consumption pattern
5. Payment acknowledged without physical receipt verification
