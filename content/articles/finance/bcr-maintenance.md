## Overview

How to maintain Budget Control Register (BCR) for AP government offices — allotment tracking, expenditure recording, reappropriation, and monthly reconciliation for Health Department institutions.

District Hospital Kurnool BCR for FY 2025-26 showed allotment of Rs. 50,00,000 under MH-251 (Drugs and Medicines). By December 2025, expenditure was Rs. 32,00,000. Smt. Lakshmi, accounts clerk, maintained daily BCR entries from CFMS payment reports.

## Applicable Rules

- AP Financial Code — Budget Control Register
- AP Budget Manual — Allotment and expenditure
- Fundamental Rules on budget authorization
- CFMS budget allotment and expenditure tracking
- Finance Department BCR maintenance instructions

## Government Orders

AP Financial Code BCR provisions on [AP Finance](https://www.apfinance.ap.gov.in/). Budget manual and CFMS expenditure reports on [cfms.ap.gov.in](https://cfms.ap.gov.in/). Budget G.Os on [GOIR](https://goir.ap.gov.in/).

## Procedure

1. Open BCR at beginning of financial year with budget allotment details
2. Record head-wise allotment received from DDO/DHO/Commissioner
3. Enter every bill payment against appropriate budget head/sub-head
4. Calculate progressive expenditure and balance available monthly
5. Verify BCR entries against CFMS expenditure reports
6. Process reappropriation requests when head-wise funds insufficient
7. Submit monthly BCR statement to controlling DDO
8. Close BCR at year-end with reconciliation certificate

## Checklist

- [ ] BCR opened with correct budget heads and allotments
- [ ] Every payment entered promptly with bill number and date
- [ ] Progressive total and balance calculated correctly
- [ ] Monthly reconciliation with CFMS done
- [ ] Expenditure not exceeding allotment without reappropriation
- [ ] Monthly BCR statement submitted to DDO
- [ ] Reappropriation orders filed and BCR updated
- [ ] Year-end reconciliation certificate prepared

## Practical Example

District Hospital Kurnool BCR for FY 2025-26 showed allotment of Rs. 50,00,000 under MH-251 (Drugs and Medicines). By December 2025, expenditure was Rs. 32,00,000. Smt. Lakshmi, accounts clerk, maintained daily BCR entries from CFMS payment reports.

Monthly reconciliation on 05-01-2026 confirmed BCR balance of Rs. 18,00,000 matched CFMS report. No overspending detected.

## Service Register Entry

Not applicable — BCR is office-level financial record, not individual SR entry.

## Common Audit Objections

1. Expenditure exceeding allotment without reappropriation
2. BCR not maintained or not updated regularly
3. BCR balance not reconciled with CFMS
4. Payments not entered in BCR promptly
5. Monthly BCR statements not submitted to controlling authority
