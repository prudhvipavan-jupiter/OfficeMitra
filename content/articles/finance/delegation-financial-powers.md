## Overview

Explanation of Delegation of Financial Powers Rules (DFPR) for AP government — who can sanction what amount, administrative vs financial approval, and application in Health Department purchases and bills.

DH Kurnool needed emergency drug purchase of Rs. 1,80,000. DFPR limits: Superintendent can sanction up to Rs. 1,00,000; DMHO up to Rs. 5,00,000.

## Applicable Rules

- AP Delegation of Financial Powers Rules (DFPR)
- AP Financial Code — Sanction authority
- G.O.Ms. on revised delegation of financial powers
- Health Department specific financial delegation orders
- Fundamental Rules on expenditure authorization

## Government Orders

Latest DFPR G.O.Ms. on [GOIR](https://goir.ap.gov.in/) and [AP Finance](https://www.apfinance.ap.gov.in/). Health Department financial delegation orders from Commissioner of Health.

## Procedure

1. Identify the transaction type — purchase, contingency, travel, medical reimbursement
2. Determine estimated value of the transaction
3. Refer to DFPR schedule for sanction authority at each amount slab
4. Obtain administrative approval from competent authority per DFPR
5. If amount exceeds DDO/Superintendent powers, route to DMHO/Commissioner
6. Record sanction authority designation and order number on bill/file
7. Verify sanction authority before CFMS bill submission
8. Maintain DFPR reference chart in accounts section for quick verification

## Checklist

- [ ] Transaction type and amount identified
- [ ] DFPR schedule consulted for correct authority
- [ ] Sanction obtained from authority competent for the amount
- [ ] Administrative approval order on file before expenditure
- [ ] Higher authority approval obtained when required
- [ ] Sanction authority recorded on bill/proposal
- [ ] CFMS bill reflects correct approving authority
- [ ] DFPR chart displayed in accounts section

## Practical Example

DH Kurnool needed emergency drug purchase of Rs. 1,80,000. DFPR limits: Superintendent can sanction up to Rs. 1,00,000; DMHO up to Rs. 5,00,000.

Superintendent administratively approved Rs. 1,00,000 portion; DMHO sanction obtained for remaining Rs. 80,000 vide Proc.No. 45/DMHO/2025. Both sanctions attached to payment bill.

## Service Register Entry

Not applicable — DFPR sanctions are office-level financial authorizations.

## Common Audit Objections

1. Expenditure sanctioned by authority not competent under DFPR
2. No administrative approval before purchase/payment
3. Split bills to stay within lower authority limits
4. DFPR limits not updated after revision G.O.
5. Sanction order missing from bill file
