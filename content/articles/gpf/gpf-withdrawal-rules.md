## Overview

Rules for GPF part-final withdrawal and partial withdrawals during service for specific purposes like marriage, education, and medical treatment under AP GPF Rules.

Smt. Lakshmi applied for GPF part-final withdrawal of Rs. 50,000 for her daughter's marriage. GPF balance: Rs. 2,80,000. Marriage invitation and relationship proof attached.

## Applicable Rules

- AP General Provident Fund Rules — Part-final withdrawal
- GPF Rules — Purpose-wise withdrawal limits
- GPF Rules — Minimum balance after withdrawal
- Finance Department GPF withdrawal procedures
- AG AP circulars on GPF part-final withdrawal

## Government Orders

AP GPF part-final withdrawal rules on AG AP portal via [AP Finance](https://www.apfinance.ap.gov.in/). Purpose-wise limits in GPF Rules circulars on GOIR.

## Procedure

1. Identify withdrawal purpose — marriage, education, medical, or other permitted purpose
2. Verify GPF account balance and minimum balance requirement after withdrawal
3. Collect application with purpose proof (marriage invitation, fee receipt, medical bills)
4. Calculate maximum permissible withdrawal for the stated purpose
5. Forward through DDO to AG/GPF authorities for sanction
6. After sanction, process disbursement through treasury/CFMS
7. Update GPF register reflecting withdrawal and reduced balance
8. No recovery required for part-final withdrawals (unlike advances)

## Checklist

- [ ] Purpose eligible under GPF Rules verified
- [ ] GPF balance sufficient including minimum balance requirement
- [ ] Supporting documents for purpose attached
- [ ] Withdrawal amount within purpose-wise limit
- [ ] AG/DDO sanction obtained
- [ ] Disbursement processed
- [ ] GPF register updated
- [ ] GPF slip issued to employee showing reduced balance

## Practical Example

Smt. Lakshmi applied for GPF part-final withdrawal of Rs. 50,000 for her daughter's marriage. GPF balance: Rs. 2,80,000. Marriage invitation and relationship proof attached.

Permissible withdrawal for marriage: up to 50% of balance = Rs. 1,40,000. Application for Rs. 50,000 forwarded and sanctioned. Disbursed through CFMS. GPF balance reduced to Rs. 2,30,000.

## Common Audit Objections

1. Withdrawal for ineligible purpose
2. Amount exceeding purpose-wise limit
3. Balance below minimum after withdrawal
4. Withdrawal without supporting documents
5. GPF register not updated after withdrawal
