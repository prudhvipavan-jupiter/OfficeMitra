## Overview

How to process GPF temporary and non-temporary advances for AP government employees — eligibility, application forms, sanction through DDO, and recovery in pay bills.

Sri Rama Rao applied for GPF temporary advance of Rs. 1,00,000 for house construction. GPF balance: Rs. 3,50,000. Smt. Lakshmi verified purpose eligibility (max 75% of balance for construction = Rs. 2,62,500).

## Applicable Rules

- AP General Provident Fund Rules
- GPF Temporary Advance Rules (purpose-wise limits)
- GPF Non-Temporary Advance Rules
- Finance Department GPF advance sanction procedures
- AG AP instructions on GPF advance recovery

## Government Orders

AP GPF Rules on AG AP portal via [AP Finance](https://www.apfinance.ap.gov.in/). GPF advance forms and purpose limits. CFMS disbursement instructions on [cfms.ap.gov.in](https://cfms.ap.gov.in/).

## Procedure

1. Verify employee is GPF subscriber with sufficient account balance
2. Identify advance type — temporary (specific purposes) or non-temporary
3. Collect application with purpose declaration and supporting documents
4. Verify advance amount within limits for stated purpose
5. Route application through DDO for sanction
6. After AG/Accounts sanction, arrange disbursement through treasury/CFMS
7. Enter recovery schedule in monthly pay bill (max 24 installments for temporary)
8. Update GPF register with advance details and recovery progress

## Checklist

- [ ] GPF account balance verified (minimum subscription period completed)
- [ ] Advance type and purpose correctly identified
- [ ] Amount within purpose-wise ceiling limits
- [ ] Application with supporting documents complete
- [ ] DDO/AG sanction obtained
- [ ] Disbursement arranged through proper channel
- [ ] Recovery started in next pay bill
- [ ] GPF register updated with advance and recovery schedule

## Practical Example

Sri Rama Rao applied for GPF temporary advance of Rs. 1,00,000 for house construction. GPF balance: Rs. 3,50,000. Smt. Lakshmi verified purpose eligibility (max 75% of balance for construction = Rs. 2,62,500).

Application forwarded to AG AP through DDO. Sanction received. Rs. 1,00,000 disbursed through CFMS. Recovery at Rs. 4,500/month for 24 months started from March 2026 pay bill.

## Common Audit Objections

1. Advance for ineligible purpose or exceeding ceiling
2. Recovery not started in pay bill after disbursement
3. Advance without AG/DDO sanction on file
4. Recovery period exceeding 24 installments for temporary advance
5. GPF register not updated with advance details
